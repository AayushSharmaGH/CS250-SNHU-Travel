## Essential Questions

**1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?**  
I interpret user needs by engaging with stakeholders through focus groups, interviews, and direct feedback. Creating user stories helps by breaking down complex requirements into simple, clear, and actionable formats (“As a [user], I want [feature] so that [benefit]”). This keeps development focused on real customer value and makes it easier to prioritize work and write acceptance criteria.

**2. How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?**  
I approach development by breaking work into small, manageable user stories and working in short iterations (sprints). In the future, I plan to incorporate Daily Scrums for coordination, Sprint Planning for clear goals, Sprint Reviews for stakeholder feedback, and Sprint Retrospectives for continuous improvement. I also want to maintain a prioritized Product Backlog and use Definition of Done to ensure quality.

**3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?**  
A good team member communicates clearly, takes ownership of their tasks, helps others when needed, and remains open to feedback. It also means being adaptable to changing requirements, attending meetings prepared, and contributing to a positive and collaborative team environment. During this course, I learned that transparency and respect are essential for team success.
